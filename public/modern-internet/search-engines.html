How Search Engines Work

Internet search engines are special sites on the Web that are designed to help people find information stored on other sites. There are three basic tasks of the search engines:


1. They search the Internet based on important words

2. They keep an index of the words they find, and where they find them.

3. They allow users to look for words or combinations of words found in that index.


At first there were not too many websites, so manpower was used for indexing. But today, with millions of webpages around the Internet, search engines now apply algorithmic indexing for an efficient and better search results.

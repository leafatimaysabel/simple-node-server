The First Internet

ARPAnet was the network that became the foundation for the Internet. It was developed under ARPA in the 1970s. ARPAnet took advantage of the TCP/IP protocols and soon enough, made possible to expand the size of the networks.


In the 1980s, ARPAnet was turned over to a separate new military network, the Defense Data Network. Becaus ARPA's name was changed to Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency or DARPA, the ARPAnet can also be called the DARPAnet.
